About Muhammad Danish

Software Engineer focused on Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Data Science. I completed my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from DHA Suffa University, Karachi, Pakistan, with a CGPA of (3.42 / 4).

My research in Bachelors includes a peer-reviewed publication titled "Speech Enhancement using Fully Convolutional UNET and Gated Convolutional Neural Network", where we proposed a method to denoise audio in its raw waveform using a 1D UNET architecture with gated linear units. This work focuses on speech enhancement techniques, providing cleaner audio for various applications.

After graduating, I worked as an AI engineer at Valyrian Systems Inc. and have won two international hackathons where I demonstrated my coding skills. I also actively teach underprivileged students through a non-profit organization, enhancing my leadership abilities.

Currently, I want to pursue a PhD to further explore AI and its applications. My goal is to contribute to impactful research, and I am eager to align my work with professors who share similar research interests, particularly in AI applications for real-world challenges.
